Why This Community Exists

The research confirms what you have been living.

After surveying more than 1,000 Black professionals across the UK, US, Canada, and Australia, the data is unambiguous. The exhaustion you feel is not a personal failing. The extra work you do to be seen as competent is real and documented. The identity suppression required to navigate predominantly white workplaces has a name: the Excellence Tax™.

The COBE Community exists because validation and strategy should not be luxuries. Because peer support from people who understand your terrain without requiring an explanation is a legitimate professional need. Because the research that names your experience should be accessible to the people living it.

What this community provides

  • Validation. Evidence that proves your experience is structural, not personal failure.
  • Strategy. Practical tools for surviving hostile workplaces or planning strategic exits.
  • Connection. Peer support from Black professionals who understand what you are navigating.
  • Education. Trauma-informed content about the Excellence Tax™ and workplace dynamics.
  • Facilitated sessions. Weekly group sessions with structured support and real-time coaching.
  • Resources. Downloadable tools you can use immediately in workplace situations.

What this community does not provide

  • Individual therapy or mental health treatment
  • Legal advice regarding employment law or discrimination claims
  • Medical advice or treatment for physical or mental health conditions
  • A guarantee of specific outcomes such as promotions, settlements, or career success
  • A substitute for professional legal, medical, or mental health support

The Practitioner Directory inside the community connects you with vetted professionals who can provide what this peer community cannot.

Who This Community Is For

This space is exclusively for Black professionals.

The research, tools, and support in this community are built around the specific lived experience of Black professionals navigating the Excellence Tax™. Maintaining this focus is what makes it a genuine sanctuary.

Eligibility Definition

This community is open to individuals who self-identify as Black, of African or Afro-Caribbean descent, who are currently employed, self-employed, or seeking employment in professional settings, and who are aged 18 or over.

We welcome Black professionals from the UK, US, Canada, Australia, and internationally. The research draws on data from all four countries and the community reflects that breadth.

Verification We reserve the right to verify eligibility. False representation of eligibility will result in immediate termination of membership without refund. This policy protects the integrity of the space for the people it was built to serve.
When to Seek Professional Support

Peer support is powerful. It is not everything.

This community provides valuable peer support and practical tools. It does not replace professional services. If you are experiencing any of the following, we encourage you to seek professional support. The Practitioner Directory inside the community can help you find culturally competent professionals.

Seek a therapist if experiencing:

  • Severe depression, anxiety, or panic attacks
  • Suicidal ideation or self-harm thoughts
  • PTSD symptoms or trauma responses
  • Burnout that interferes with daily functioning

Seek legal advice if experiencing:

  • Active discrimination or harassment claims
  • Potential constructive dismissal situations
  • Settlement negotiations with your employer
  • Concerns about your employment contract or rights

Seek medical care if experiencing:

  • Physical symptoms such as chronic pain or sleep disorders
  • Need for sick leave or disability accommodations
  • Health conditions exacerbated by workplace stress
  • Medication management needs

Participation in this community does not create a therapist-client, solicitor-client, or doctor-patient relationship. Content shared is educational and for general support purposes only.

Confidentiality
